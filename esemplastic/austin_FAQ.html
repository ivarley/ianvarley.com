FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions) About Ian Moving To Austin

FAQ

Q: You're moving to Austin?!?

A: Yes.

Q: When are you moving to Austin??

A: June 2005.

Q: Why are you moving to Austin?

A: Well, my wife has been in graduate school at UH for 3 years in an MFA writing program. She graduated last month, and wanted to get a job as a book editor. We were starting to think we'd have to relocate to New York in order to get a job in publishing, since most of the jobs are there. But then she got a great job offer in Austin, which is a town we've always loved. She took it and we are relocating, just like that.

Q: What's going to happen to your band?

A: Nothing much will change for the band. As it is, we're already playing out of Houston (all over Texas, Oklahoma, Louisiana, etc.) more than we are playing in Houston (see graph). Ideally, we'd love to be playing about once a month in Houston, and the rest of the time be out on the road. We're still a Houston-based band, though ... we rehearse together (in Houston) and we'll be starting work on our new record (at SugarHill Studios, in Houston) later this summer. We collaborate on songwriting over the internet, and then come together once or twice a month for marathon rehearsal sessions where we put things together.

Q: What are you going to do for work?

A: I'm actually fortunate enough to be keeping the job I already have, which is as a computer programmer for a company in Houston. I will be working from home in Austin most of the time, with trips in to the Houston office for meetings every couple weeks. This is pretty great, since (as a programmer) there's not much point in my being in a cube all day. In fact, productivity is way up (see graph).

Q: What's going to happen to the Houston Music Roundtable now that you're gone?

A: The Houston Music Roundtable will continue unabated. We've already changed to a once-a-month model for the roundtable, which I plan on coming out for every month. I'm also changing things so that other people can start to take a more active role (i.e. changing to a 3rd party list system, etc.). To paraphrase John Lomax, hopefully the institution has grown bigger than the people who run it.

I'll also likely be starting some similar activities in Austin, and if all goes well, I'd love to encourage more cross pollenation between the two cities.

Q: Are you still going to do a jazz show on KTRU?

A: Not regularly, no. I may still do one now and again, but Sunday nights are the toughest to be in Houston.

Q: Why haven't you returned my email?

A: Perhaps another graph will illustrate. As you can see, there are 200+ emails from the past month alone that I need to reply to. With all the craziness of moving, I've been getting way behind. I'll remedy that, but give me a few weeks. If it's really important, don't hesitate to email again.

Q: What part of Austin do you live in?

A: I live in the Clarksville neighborhood in central Austin, just north of town lake around Lamar Blvd (a couple blocks from Waterloo records). If you're coming through Austin, drop me a line and perhaps I can offer you a place to crash.

Q: Do you like Austin better than Houston?

A: So far I like 'em about equal ... but then, I'm a big fan of Houston's many hidden treasures ... Brasil, Last Concert Cafe, Late Night Pie, etc. Austin definitely has it over Houston as far as general environment goes (a lot less smoggy & ugly than Houston), but Houston is a little more easygoing (so far, anyway). So there you have it. Check my blog if you want to know how things are going.

Any other questions? Just email me.
